Why I love Airbnb

In October 2012, I embarked on a small EuroTrip that would last three months. I decided to give Airbnb a try. I ended up loving the experience of finding and booking flats. It felt secure and intuitive, as if that’s the default way to book accommodation when traveling.

Fast forward to 2013. To whomever I tell about my trip, they stare at me surprised and ask: “How could you trust booking with a host, in a foreign country?”. That’s when I tell them about you guys, and I realise that a well designed experience, has the ability to convey trust and persuade you into doing things you would otherwise avoid.

Who am I?

I am a designer and developer who creates engaging experiences for the web. I am intrigued by responsive websites, clean and semantic code, and pixel-perfect designs.

I work at SicanStudios, my online corner from where I craft web experiences for clients. Some call it “freelancing”. I call it “the best way to learn and become a pro”. Freelancing has shaped me into this “MacGyver” of the web. To survive as a freelancer, you have to be good at many things; from sketching, to designing and coding, to being organised and self-motivated.

I know that my time on this planet is limited, so I always strive to learn more, influence more, and get better. I want to keep the pressure up. I want to take on the toughest labours, and challenge the status quo. I believe Airbnb is the next place for me to do that.

Who are you?

You are Airbnb. You were recently valued at $1.3 billion. Every two seconds someone books a room with you. When hurricane Sandy hit, you helped people find shelter. Daily, you impact the lives of hundreds of thousands of people. You might just be the next big thing.

That’s why you need the best. You need a MacGyver who is not intimidated by your success, nor is afraid to get his hands dirty; one who will not only implement and improve the visual language of Airbnb but will also help visualise the future of travel.

I’m that guy. I love challenges. I love the idea of working with brilliant people, whom I admire and respect, on a product I use and love. I know the hard work, dedication, and teamwork it takes to create something great, and I look forward to making Airbnb even better with my addition.

P.S. Did you know this site is responsive? I spend time refining and optimising the experience, so it should look great on a tablet or mobile.
