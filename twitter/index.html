I think we should talk.

Let’s talk about me

I am a web designer and developer who creates engaging experiences for the web. I am intrigued by responsive websites, clean and semantic code, and pixel-perfect designs.

Blame my character, but there are a million things I want to try, to do, and to get better at. When not designing, I write about coding and design, I take photographs, and make videos & animations. I also love playing football, my bass guitar, and travelling—just returned from a three month trip across Europe. There’s never enough time for everything, though I’m really trying.

I work at SicanStudios, which is my own portal from where I craft web experiences for clients and for fun. Some call it “freelancing”. I call it “the best way to learn and become a pro”. Freelancing has shaped me into this “MacGyver” of the web. To survive as a freelancer, you have to be good at many things from designing and coding, to being organised and self-motivated.

SicanStudios is a great place to work. I pursue only exciting projects, I have creative freedom, and the salary is good. I cannot think of anything that would keep me from working here a very long time. But I always want more. Not necessarily stuff or money, but I want to learn more, influence more, and get better. I want to keep the pressure up. I want to take on the toughest labours, and challenge the status quo.

Let’s talk about you

You are Twitter. With just 140 characters you managed to attract millions of people, world leaders, and celebrities to use your social network. And we prefer you over other networks. You have created a world-class, engaging, and addictive product that has radically changed the world.

That’s why you need the best. You need a MacGyver who is not intimidated by your success, nor is afraid to get his hands dirty; one who will improve and help define the Twitter experience.

I’ll be honest. I didn’t make this page lightly, and I’ve spent some time considering whether or not to really go for this. This is a high-profile job at a company that is put on a pedestal by the web community. Creating interactive experiences across platforms that will be used by many, and acting as an evangelist for UI best practices is not a stroll in the park— it’s a tough challenge. This job requires superb organisational skills, crazy attention to detail, and brass balls.

I’m that guy. I love challenges. I love the idea of working with brilliant people, whom I admire and respect, on a product I use daily. I know the hard work and dedication that it takes to create something great, and I look forward to making Twitter better with my addition to the flock.
